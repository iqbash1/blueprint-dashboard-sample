How this works

Two questions

Results — is Hawaiʻi's economy getting better for the people who live here? Implementation — are the 17 Blueprint actions getting done? Every measure shows what it counts, where it started, the latest number, its source, and when it updates.

State rankings

A ranking tab appears only where every state is measured the same way, so comparisons are fair. Measures with no fair national comparison (like visitor arrivals) don't get one.

The arrows

Arrows show only which way a number is moving — rising, falling, or flat. They don't say good or bad, because the same change can help some people and hurt others: rising home prices favor owners and burden buyers. Saying whether Hawaiʻi is "on track" would need targets, and those haven't been set yet.

Smoothing

Chart lines are 3-month averages, so single-month swings don't distract. Headline numbers and tables show the exact official monthly figures.

Where the data comes from

  • Everything on this page comes from official public sources — the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the Census Bureau, and Zillow's public research data — and anyone can re-create it.
  • The full version adds three more kinds of data, each clearly labeled: readings of public records (like bills), data shared by partners (like UH or the schools), and reports from the people responsible for each action.

Keep in mind

  • Government data runs one to three months behind; some yearly data runs a year or two behind.
  • Past numbers get revised; this page shows what was published as of the date above.
  • Zillow's home-value figure is an estimate — used because no government source publishes one monthly for every state.
  • Business applications count paperwork filed, not businesses that actually open.
